Pinagrealan Cave

This cave is a subterranean network of caverns extending more than a kilometer deep. The Katipunero Revolutionaries during the war against Spain used it as a camp in 1896 and again during the Filipino-American War in 1898 as hideout of General Emilio Aguinaldo (the First President of the Philippines). It was also used as a sanctuary by the Japanese Imperial Army when the Philippines was liberated by American Forces.

What to Know?

Activities

There is so many activities that you can enjoy in this area. From mountain climbing, trekking, hikking, and specialy Cave Exploration.
 
 
 

Delicacies

Norzagaray is blessed with natural beauty and is also known for their native kakanins or rice delicacies. It's sweet and sticky rice delicacies called "Casava Cake".
 

Hotels

The nearest hotels that you can find in the area is the "Hotel Turista" at San Jose Del Monte Bulacan.
 

Transportation

If you're from Manila then you'll need to use Busses to go to Norzagaray, Bulacan then use Tricicle to be able to go to the Cave
 
 

Expences

You'll need to have at least P3,000 - P4,000 pocket money for a trip in Pinagrealan Cave. This will include hotels, Foods and more.
 
 

Animals

Animals sight-seeing is not a problem in this area. You'll definitely enjoy to see a wild forest animals.
